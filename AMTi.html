The Problem

Old Metal, New Process

Titanium has been around for ages, but additive manufacturing seeks to revolutionize how it's made. By manufacturing through addtiion rather than subtraction, metal waste is reduced and more intricate geometries can be acheived.

Adapting

New Manufacturing, New Issues

Using AM has many advantages, but also poses challenges in the form of manufacturing issues such as hot cracking, where micro-cracks and other flaws appear within the sample and lower its performance. In order to get around this, the alloy composition must be carefully considered.

Computational Design

Determining Composition

By running calculations of alloy properties that correspond to the susceptibility of a sample to these problems, we are able to determine the ideal composition. By slightly varying each composition paramater individually we are able to find a composition that is compatible with the AM process.

Results

What We Came Up With

At the end of our project, we were able to suggest two specific alloy compositions that would be able to be additively manufactured without creating hot cracking while also maintaining the baseline properties required for most applications. These results were presented to the client who was able to implement them.
