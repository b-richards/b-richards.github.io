Who is she?

Uncovering 1300 year old secrets

Little is known about the Hibbard Mummy, a 1300 year old Portrait Mummy from Egypt. Who is the mummy? How did they die? What were they like in life? As part of an interdisciplinary team, I helped to try to uncover a little bit more about this mummy.

Picture a Mummy

Modern Techniques meet Ancient Mummy

Using Visible Infrared Spectroscopy (VILS), MRI Imaging, and sampling material from the mummy, we were able to determine the mummy’s origin from the Fayum depression in Egypt. We determined the mummy to be that of a girl who died from an unknown cause around the age of 8.

Craft

What we found

Additional imaging showed the use of many other pigments that have been known to have been used throughout Roman Egypt. Reflectance Transformation Imaging revealed the deeper topography of the portrait. This data, along with trends of mummy portraiture, suggest the portrait was created using the encaustic method, which involved mixing pigment with hot wax and manipulating it with heated tools. If the portrait is in fact encaustic, this may suggest that perhaps the girl’s death was expected.

Identity

So who is she?

We might never know who she is, but my work helped determine that the mummy was that of an 8 year old girl. Additionally, I helped to uncover the use of Egyptian Blue within the portrait. The use of the world's first artifical pigment was unknown in this historic context making a question for future exploration.
