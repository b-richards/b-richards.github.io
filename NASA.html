Building Blocks

It's All in the Powder

Not much is known about how small variances in the powder of an additively manufactured metal change the finished products properties. As part of a research team at the NASA Glenn Research Center, I helped to characterize how small changes had large impacts on finished products.

What's that?

Small Impurities, Large Impact

Using scanning electron micrsocopy (SEM) and optical microscopy, I identified impurities within fractured samples and determined the impact they had on the sample through fracture analysis. One such impurity is shown here. The golden nitride may look pretty, but actually has a negative impact on the part performance.

Breaking it down

What we found

After examining over 100 samples that failed under cyclic stress conditions (fatigue failure), we were able to determine that surface defects on the samples were the primary cause of sample failure. These artefacts unique to the AM process pose a challenge to future work in the field to create AM metal parts.
